### Session Highlights

**Session 1 – Huỳnh Thái Linh: Level Up Your AWS Skills with Cloud Quest and Floci**
- Showed newcomers how to get past the anxiety around unexpected AWS costs and resource management
- Introduced AWS Cloud Quest, a free hands-on platform for learning AWS through real labs
- Introduced Floci, a simulation tool for testing out architectures without incurring real costs

**Session 2 – "The Ballers" team: The Hackathon Journey**
- Recounted building two real products in a tight timeframe:
  - SynthHunter, an AI-based voice verification system
  - Vortex, a platform connecting people through the hiring journey
- Key lesson: learning by doing, and staying persistent under deadline pressure

**Session 3 – Nguyễn Thị Quỳnh Như: Why We Always Need Confidence**
- Argued that confidence is essential for both career growth and university life
- Pointed out hidden barriers — self-doubt, fear of judgment — often masked by a quiet exterior
- Offered practical ways to work past fear and build lasting confidence

**Session 4 – Nghĩa Trần: Building the "Tử Vi Đại Việt" Startup Platform**
- Introduced an AI platform combining traditional Vietnamese astrology (Tử Vi Đẩu Số) and Bát Tự to make fate reading more accessible and systematic
- Targeted Gen Z users with an affordable pricing model
- Shared the plan to migrate the infrastructure to AWS for better scalability and cost efficiency

**Session 5 – Trần Minh Quân: The Hidden Iceberg of a Project: DevOps Before Disaster**
- Warned about the hidden risks in software projects — like the submerged part of an iceberg — lurking behind seemingly simple features
- Described the consequences of a weak DevOps process: production bugs, missed deadlines, and burned-out teams
- Emphasized the importance of CI/CD, monitoring, and early testing

**Session 6 – Khôi Phạm: The Iceberg of Procrastination**
- Argued that procrastination isn't laziness — it stems from hidden fears (fear of failure, fear of judgment)
- Used psychological research to explain the root causes of procrastination
- Encouraged starting with small actions to build momentum and confidence

---

### What I Learned

**Technical skills**
- Learned to use AWS Cloud Quest to practice AWS for free, without worrying about unexpected charges
- Got a clearer picture of the real product-building process in a hackathon: ideation → prototype → demo
- Recognized how important DevOps is for catching project risks early

**Soft skills**
- Gained a clearer view of how confidence shapes personal and career growth
- Understood the roots of procrastination and how small, consistent actions can overcome it
- Picked up on the persistence and adaptability shown by "The Ballers" team

**Entrepreneurial mindset**
- Saw how blending AI with traditional culture can produce a distinctive product, as with Tử Vi Đại Việt
- Understood why choosing the right infrastructure platform (like AWS) matters for a product's ability to scale

---

### Personal Reflections

Saturday Meet Up stood out for how many different angles it covered — AWS engineering, the hackathon mindset, psychology, and entrepreneurship all in one morning.

What made it memorable was the range of topics: not just technology, but real psychology and real founder stories, which gave attendees a broader lens on personal growth.

The Ballers' hackathon story was especially inspiring — despite time pressure and setbacks, the team shipped their projects and walked away with valuable lessons. Nghĩa Trần's talk also showed that AI can be applied creatively to traditional domains, opening up a distinctive path for young people looking to start something of their own.

The talks on confidence and procrastination were also a chance to reflect on my own hidden fears and find extra motivation to act.

---

### Key Takeaways

- Learning by doing beats theory alone — AWS Cloud Quest and Floci are great starting points.
- DevOps isn't optional; it's a requirement for a project's long-term success.
- Confidence, paired with small actions, is the key to overcoming procrastination and fear.
- Young builders can absolutely blend technology and culture to create something genuinely valuable.
